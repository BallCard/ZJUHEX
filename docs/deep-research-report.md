## Cross-document deduplication and knowledge integration

The deduplication problem breaks into three separate layers: exact duplicates, near-lexical duplicates, and semantic duplicates. You should not solve all three with embeddings alone. The best open-source design I found is a staged pipeline: exact/hash filtering first, MinHash or SimHash second, and embedding-based clustering or ANN matching third. ŅłĆurlŅłéChenghaoMou/text-dedupŅłéturn9search5Ņłü already gives you MinHash, MinHashLSH, SimHash, substring deduplication, and Bloom-filter exact deduplication in a configurable package, while ŅłĆurlŅłéfacebookresearch/faissŅłéturn9search6Ņłü provides the scalable dense-vector search layer for semantic similarity. ŅłĆentityŅłé["software","Sentence Transformers","embedding and reranking library"]Ņłü fills the middle with paraphrase mining, semantic search, and community detection. ŅłĆciteŅłéturn10view4Ņłéturn10view3Ņłéturn10view1Ņłü

The most useful threshold guidance I found comes from the defaults and examples of the upstream tools themselves. `text-dedup` ships a MinHash example with a threshold of `0.7`, which is a sensible starting point for lexical near-duplicates. Sentence TransformersŌĆÖ `community_detection` defaults to a cosine threshold of `0.75`, which is a good first-pass cluster threshold for semantically similar passages. ŅłĆurlŅłéSemHash deduplication docsŅłéturn11view0Ņłü default to `0.9` for semantic deduplication and include tools such as `get_least_similar_from_duplicates()` and `rethreshold()` specifically to tune borderline cases. That last range is my synthesis from the toolsŌĆÖ defaults rather than a universal law, because chunk size, heading retention, and embedding model choice all shift the score distribution.
